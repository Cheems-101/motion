MotionCOT: Frame-Based Multi-Scale Reasoning for Human Motion Generation

Under Review 2024

The goal of text-driven human motion synthesis is to generate corresponding 3D human motion sequences based on a given text, which has important applications in many fields such as gaming, robotics, and digital humans.Recent work has demonstrated that language models have made remarkable progress in generating human motion.However, these methods are still constrained by the problems of inefficient training and accumulated errors during inference. To address these issues, we introduce MotionCOT, an efficient motion generation framework. Through the proposed frame-based multi-scale reasoning method, MotionCOT first predicts coarse-grained motion subsequences, which then progressively guide the model to reasoning more fine-grained motion sequences. Through our analysis, this multi-scale reasoning mechanism not only ensures adequate training but also avoids accumulated errors during inference. Furthermore, we propose a preference-optimized keyframe reasoning method, which evaluates the sampled motion sequences through multi-grained views, and then uses the Direct Preference Optimization method~(DPO) to optimize the model to generate high-score motions. Our experiments show that MotionCOT significantly outperforms existing models, particularly in terms of motion diversity and long-sequence motion generation.
